Installation Project

Introduction

This was a 5 person project developed over 5 weeks for a course called Interactive Arts. The task was to develop an interactive art installation. This piece is called Invisible Touch. I took on a leadership role, I proposed the idea and worked as the main project director.

Process

Invisible Touch was inspired by Telematic Dreaming (1992) by Paul Sermon. Below are some of the first sketches I drew of the concept. The installation uses two cameras and two projectors that display the camera's live footage. The projector displays are overlapped such that, when people put their hands in front of the two separate cameras, the display will show them touching. You can see this shown roughly in the left sketch, and on the right is an initial drawing of the camera box setup.

To test the technology for the project, we rented out all the necessary equipment from the library. We connected the cameras to the projectors and got the projector images to overlap by simply stacking the projectors on top of one another.

There were a few things that we changed in the following test. I learned more about the camera settings to turn off any interface displays (white boxes, text). We also rented bigger projectors and a proper projector screen, and constructed our black boxes for people to put their hands into. This allowed us to see something akin to what our final product would actually look like.

Reflection

Invisible Touch is a conceptual piece about space and the human body. While participantsâ€™ images may touch, they physically remain separated in space in their respective boxes. By isolating your body into one of these boxes in order to connect digitally, Invisible Touch explores the extent to which digitally-mediated closeness is possible, and what closeness can feel like in these spaces. Be it the growing normality of technology or a pandemic-induced necessity for it, the conversation of digital closeness is more relevant than ever.

While this project definitely does reach broader universal themes, it carries a lot of meaning to me on a personal level too. As a trans person, I think this piece explains something about my emotional disconnect with my body and my desire to connect with people and feel present in those experiences.
